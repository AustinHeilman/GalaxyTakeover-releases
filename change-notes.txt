GalaxyTakeover - Change Notes
==============================

[April 7 2026]

Diplomacy
---------
- New "Demand Surrender" diplomacy option. AI empires with overwhelming
  military advantage (5x strength, target ≤3 colonies) can demand
  surrender. Surrendering empire transfers all colonies and units.
- AI races now have distinct diplomatic personalities — opinion
  thresholds, cooldowns, war triggers, and peace evaluation are all
  configurable per race in ai-personality.json.
- AI will demand surrender from weakened enemies and evaluate surrender
  demands using race-specific thresholds (never, homeworld_lost, or
  numeric strength ratio).
- Scriptable diplomacy evaluation: four new events (treaty.evaluate,
  war.consider, peace.evaluate, surrender.evaluate) let race scripts
  override all diplomatic decisions with per-race logic.

AI Fleet Composition
--------------------
- AI now builds supply tenders and repair ships to extend fleet
  range and survivability. Support ships are produced at ratios
  (1 supply per 12 warships, 1 repair per 15) once 10+ warships exist.
- AI composes mixed fleets with support ships instead of pure warship
  blobs. Support ships are merged into combat groups at rally points
  so supply redistribution and fleet repair actually work.
- New design recipes for fleet support roles (supply tender, repair ship).
  ShipDesignMinister auto-designs these when tech is available.
- Design recipe system now supports exact component selection via
  componentId field for modders (e.g., specify repair bays without
  accidentally picking up space yards).

Warp System
-----------
- Fix: Fleets blocked from warping by hostile forces at warp points.
  Warp is a grid-space transition — combat triggers on entering the
  tile, not when executing the warp. Removed incorrect interception
  check that permanently blocked warps when enemies were present.
- Fix: Solo units stuck at warp points during multi-system moves.
  ExecutePendingWarp now falls back to MovementPlan.CurrentLeg.WarpPointId
  when PendingWarpPointId is cleared between turns.
- Design rule documented in code and CombatNotes.md: warp follows
  the same arrival-based combat rules as all other grid movement.

Dynamic Movement
----------------
- New: Destination-only movement mode for cross-system moves. Units
  store a destination and dynamically A* pathfind each turn, adapting
  to warp topology changes (new/destroyed warp points). Both modes
  coexist — intra-system moves use pre-computed paths, cross-system
  player moves default to dynamic routing.
- Follows the same pattern as AttackTargetId pursuit: store target,
  repath each turn via UpdateDynamicMovementPaths.
- Persists across save/load (DestinationSystemId/DestinationPosition
  on IUnit and IUnitGroup).

Combat System
-------------
- Fix: Colony re-bombardment every turn. Fleets parked on enemy
  colonies no longer auto-bombard — bombardment happens on arrival
  only. To bombard again, issue an explicit attack command or move
  away and back. Matches design rule: combat triggers on arrival.
- Fix: Intercept orders no longer re-trigger combat every turn when
  the unit is at the target position but didn't move. Units blockade
  instead of re-engaging. Explicit EngageAtPosition (attack command)
  is required for intentional re-engagement.
- AI fleets at enemy colonies now use EngageAtPosition to re-engage
  defenders, matching the arrival-only combat rule.

Attack Targeting (UI)
---------------------
- Fix: Clicking Attack then clicking a colony with a fleet on top
  now correctly targets the colony (prioritizes colonies over units).
- Fix: Attack mode now checks diplomatic hostility — only hostile
  entities are valid targets (prevents attacking allies).
- New: Selection panel (right-side object list) supports attack mode.
  Click a hostile entity in the list to issue attack commands directly.
- Fix: Attacking an enemy group now works — resolves group to its
  first unit as a stable pursuit target.

Ghost Unit Cleanup
------------------
- Fix: 809+ ghost unit duplicates found in saves. After save/load,
  WarpUnitToSystem used reference-equality Remove which silently
  failed (different object instances with same ID). Changed to
  ID-based lookup for removal.
- Added DeduplicateSystemObjects at movement phase start to clean
  up existing ghosts from old saves.
- Fix: Presence indicators (galaxy map) now filter destroyed units
  from system queries.

[April 6 2026]

Combat
------
- Fix: Weapon mounts now correctly apply damage scaling, range
  bonuses, and accuracy bonuses during combat. Previously, a
  heavy-mounted weapon did the same damage as an unmounted one.
  This was a critical bug — capital ships and battle stations
  with large mounts were no stronger than small ships.
- Mount scaling reference:
  - Ship mounts: Standard 1x, Large 2x, Heavy 3x, Massive 5x
  - Base mounts: Standard 1x, Large 3x, Heavy 5x, Massive 7x
  - Range and accuracy bonuses also scale with mount size.

AI Ship Design
--------------
- New design recipe system: each race can define ship design
  rules in a design-recipes.json file. Recipes specify weapon
  preferences, engine requirements, defense ratios, and role-
  specific components (mine bays, fighter bays, etc.). Gives
  each race distinct ship design identity without ML training.
- AI ships now prioritize engines. Previously, the AI would
  build ships with only 1 engine, making them too slow to
  reach enemies or bombard colonies. Ships now have 2-4
  engines depending on hull size.

AI Fleet Movement
-----------------
- AI fleets now move and warp correctly as groups. Previously,
  individual ships in a fleet could get stuck or take different
  paths.
- AI fleets in the same system as an enemy now attack instead
  of sitting idle.
- Solo ships use proper pathfinding and warp correctly.
- Clearing orders on a fleet now clears orders on all ships
  in the fleet.

Mod System
----------
- Mod content hash: a SHA256 fingerprint is computed after mods
  load. Save files store the hash and warn on load if mods have
  changed.
- ML training workers now verify both DLL and mod content hashes
  match the server before training, preventing stale worker
  mismatches.

Stability
---------
- Save files with duplicate unit IDs no longer crash on load.
- Cleaned up stale per-ship movement data that could conflict
  with fleet-level orders.

[April 3 2026]

Combat & Bombardment
--------------------
- AI fleets now properly bombard enemy colonies after winning space
  combat. Previously, ships with regular weapons wouldn't fire on
  colonies even when no defenders remained.
- Bombardment damage increased — orbital strikes are now devastating
  to colony populations, matching what a human player would expect.
- Combat outcome feedback: AI empires now know how many colonies
  they bombarded, population they killed, and facilities they
  destroyed. Defenders know what they lost too.

AI Ship Design
--------------
- AI empires now build bigger ships as tech allows. Previously they
  were capped at light cruisers regardless of available research.
  Expect to see battleships, juggernauts, and baseships in mid-to-late
  game.
- AI threat adaptation: empires now react to what's killing them.
  Hit mines? Build mine sweepers. Facing fighters? Add point defense.
  Shields blocking everything? Research better weapons. Ships getting
  captured? Add security stations. Responses escalate with repeated
  encounters.

AI Fleet Behavior
-----------------
- Solo ship consolidation: newly built warships now join nearby
  groups instead of flying solo. Idle solos in the same system
  form up into proper fleets. Large empires consolidate more
  aggressively.
- Weapon evaluation API added for ship design — AI can now query
  which weapons deal the most damage on which mounts at any range,
  instead of guessing.

AI Race Identity
----------------

  doctrines: Padario (shock and awe), Bodzeen (carrier strike
  groups), Harmari (intelligence warfare), Melians (fortress
  defense), Sijhai (tech supremacy), Takirot (space base
  fortifications), Sandifer (expendable swarms), Kollekt (armored
  brawlers), and Aleeman (stealth mines and boarding).

UI
--
- Facility build queue now warns when a system-wide facility (like
  hospitals) is already built or queued elsewhere in the same system.

Balance
-------
- Hospitals expanded from 3 tiers to 5, matching plague bomb levels.
- Space base initial research cost reduced.

Infrastructure
--------------
- TurnProcessor split into 10 partial class files for easier
  maintenance.
- Modding docs moved to ship with DevTools so modders have them
  alongside the tools.

[April 2 2026 – Update 2]

Diplomacy
---------
- Treaties can now be broken. Empires can unilaterally withdraw from
  a treaty or declare war; the change takes effect when the turn is
  processed (not immediately). Treaty-breaking can be disabled per

- Fix AreHostile checking only one side of a diplomatic relation.
  Previously the result depended on argument order — if one empire
  had ignoreCombat and the other didn't, units could share a grid
  position without combat. Both directions are now checked.

Movement & Combat
-----------------
- Fix BattleQueue re-fighting the same position every movement step.
  Per-step tracking now ensures combat only triggers for fresh
  arrivals, not positions where a unit moved on a prior step.
- Fix stale wave cleanup in BattleQueue.Drain — waves that produced
  no combat were never removed, inflating iteration counts on
  subsequent calls.
- Fix bombardment edge cases from prior simplification: 0-pop
  colonies with no facilities now yield instant attacker victory
  instead of 30 empty rounds; unarmed troop carriers now enter the
  combat loop so they can reach the colony before deploying troops.

Scripting & AI
--------------
- Fix BaseAIHandler script calling Log() with 2 arguments (the mod
  scripting API only exposes the 1-arg overload).
- Diagnostic logging added for multi-empire no-hostile positions.

Infrastructure
--------------
- Release workflow now supports manual runs via workflow_dispatch.
- Selfplay temp dirs and *.db added to .gitignore.
- Branch cleanup script uses reset --hard to handle rewritten history.

- Optimal engagement range and kiting added to combat movement. Ships

- Attackers no longer spawn on top of the colony in combat replays.

[March 29-31 2026]

Combat Balance
--------------
- Point-Defense rebalanced: damage curve 20 → 100 across 8 tiers
  (was 10 → 20 across 5 tiers). Three new tiers (T6-T8) added.
  PD accuracy boosted at lower tiers (70% → 85% at T1).
  Point-Defense now properly shreds seekers and fighters at all tiers.
- Point-Defense targeting fixed: maxTargetTonnage was never enforced
  (always returned 0 for target tonnage). PD now correctly reads
  chassis mass — can only target units ≤ 150 kT (corvettes, fighters,
  seekers, drones, satellites). Cannot target frigates or larger.
- Armor HP reduced across all families for better shield parity:
  Standard armor: 50/125/200/275/350/425 (was 100/200/300/400/500/600).
  Emissive armor: 75/150/225 (was 200/300/400).
  Stealth armor: 75/150/225 (was 150/200/250).
  Scattering armor: 75/150/225 (was 200/300/400).
  Armor still wins single fights with higher HP/mass, but shields
  now compete by regenerating between combats.

Stellar Manipulation

[March 28 2026]

Combat Interception Fixes
-------------------------
- Fixed warp point combat bypass: units that arrived at a warp point
  on a previous turn could warp through unchallenged, even with enemy
  warships guarding the point. Now hostile presence unconditionally
  blocks warping on both departure and arrival sides.
- Fixed colonization bypass: colony ships could colonize planets at
  grid positions defended by enemy weapon platforms or fighters. Now
  colonies with weapon platforms or deployable combat units block
  hostile colonization. Undefended colonies correctly do NOT block.
- 19 warp combat tests + 10 colonization combat tests added as
  regression suite covering war, peace, and alliance states.

Ground Combat & Colony Capture
------------------------------
- Fixed population zeroing on colony capture. Militia death formula
  (killed² × 1M + 10% war casualties) could exceed total population.
  Pop loss now capped at 50% on capture, 25% on repelled invasion.
- Militia capped at 50 per colony (was unlimited — 32B pop = 179 militia).
- Captured colonies now properly transfer weapon platforms, cargo, and
  population groups to the new owner. Previously weapon platforms showed
  "?" icons after capture.
- Troop survivor count fixed — was inflating by returning all troops
  from non-destroyed stacks instead of trimming to actual survivors.
- Ground combat balance values (militia cap, war casualties, pop loss
  caps) now moddable via game-settings.json in basegame-troops package.

Ungrouped Unit Bug Fix

[March 27 2026]

Combat Engine Stability — OOM & Stalemate Fixes
------------------------------------------------
- Fixed exponential memory growth caused by weapon platform cargo
  duplication. Colony weapon platforms doubled in memory every combat,
  eventually consuming 6GB+. Weapon platforms now live in a dedicated
  colony.WeaponPlatforms collection instead of cycling through cargo.
- Fixed troop carrier invasion loop — troop carriers would repeat
  "approaching colony" forever without landing. The no-weapons stalemate
  check was skipping the entire combat loop, preventing troops from
  deploying. Troops now land correctly at hostile colonies.
- Fixed bombardment being cut short — the zero-damage stalemate detector
  only tracked ship-to-ship damage, so bombardment progress (destroying
  facilities, killing population) was invisible. Bombardment weapons
  (Planetary Napalm, Neutron Bombs, plague bombs) now work correctly.
- Layered stalemate detection: scripts can now vote via combat.can_begin
  event before combat grid is even created. Engine checks broadened to
  detect bombardment weapons and warheads alongside regular weapons.
- 800-turn selfplay games now complete in 2-6 minutes, under 1.1GB peak
  memory. Previously OOM'd at 6GB+ around turn 100.

Combat Event Optimization
-------------------------
- Combat events (weapon firing, hit calculating, missed, etc.) are now
  skipped entirely when no mod handlers are listening. Saves ~570MB of
  GameEvent allocations per combat-heavy turn in selfplay.
- Added HasHandlers() to the event dispatcher for O(1) handler existence
  checks. Combat scripts use this to skip event construction.
- Replay effects only created when replays are being captured. No wasted
  allocations in headless/selfplay mode.
- Combat XP only awarded after decisive victories, not stalemates.
  Eliminated 210K+ spurious XP events per game.

Fleet Consolidation
-------------------
- AI fleets now consolidate into larger strike forces before attacking.
  Solo ships rally to staging depots instead of exploring during war.
- Minimum attack fleet size enforced (8 ships, 5 for aggressive races).
  No more suicidal 3-ship raids against 40-colony empires.
- New fleets hold at depot until 15+ ships before being assigned attack
  missions. Builds decisive force instead of scattering immediately.

Weapon Platforms Refactored
---------------------------
- Weapon platforms are now permanent colony installations stored in a
  dedicated collection, not deployed/retrieved from cargo each combat.
- Production delivers weapon platforms directly to the colony's defense
  collection instead of generic cargo stockpile.
- Colony detail UI shows weapon platforms alongside cargo items.

Explore Command
---------------
- New "Explore" command available on all ships with engines. Automatically
  finds the nearest warp point to unexplored space using BFS pathfinding.
- Appears under the Move dropdown in the ship command bar.
- Works for both player clicks and AI dispatch via the same event system.

Ship Command Bar — Dynamic Sub-Commands
----------------------------------------
- Built-in command buttons (Move, Warp, Attack, Resupply, Repair) now
  support dynamic sub-command dropdowns from component definitions.
- Mod-defined commands with matching group names automatically appear
  under the correct built-in button's flyout.

Selfplay Training Improvements
------------------------------
- Combat metrics now tracked in SQLite: per-combat timing, stalemate
  rates, round counts, attacker/defender counts, system positions.
- Setup phase instrumentation identifies slow combat initialization.
- Games run 800 turns (was 250), 15-minute timeout (was 5).
- OOM analysis instructions added — selfplay Claude investigates memory
  spikes and writes findings to engine-issues.md.
- Diplomatic history capped at 50 entries per relation to prevent
  unbounded growth.
- Message retention reduced to 2 turns in selfplay (was 10).
- Stale AIState exploration keys cleaned up per turn.
- Event dispatcher metrics and script profiler reset every 50 turns.

[February 19 2026]

Special Solar Systems
---------------------
Four new hazardous system types can now appear on the galaxy map, shown as
diamond shapes instead of the usual circles:

- Dead Systems: Cold, dim stellar cores surrounded by asteroid belts. No
  habitable planets, but good for mining. Up to 3 per galaxy.

- Pulsar Systems: Neutron stars with rotating radiation beams. Ships and
  colonies caught in the beams take 25 damage per turn, and colonies lose
  5% population. The beams rotate each turn so safe positions change.
  Up to 2 per galaxy.

- Magnetar Systems: Neutron stars that build up magnetic charge over time.
  When charge reaches critical levels, a devastating surge fires — dealing
  up to 60 damage with falloff by distance and wiping out up to 20% of
  colony population in range. Watch for the growing pink glow as a warning.
  At most 1 per galaxy.

- Nebula Systems: Dense, colorful gas clouds that severely reduce scanner
  range (~15% of normal). Great for ambushes, dangerous to navigate blind.
  Six color variants. Up to 3 per galaxy.

Cloaking
--------
- New Cloaking Device components (Tiers I-III, requires Cloaking research)
- Toggle cloak on/off with the K key when a cloaked ship is selected
- Cloaked ships consume supplies each turn to maintain the cloak
- Ships automatically decloak when firing weapons in combat
- New Anti-Cloak Sensors (Tiers I-III) let you detect cloaked enemies
- Scanner Jamming: units with ECM can block enemy scanner sweeps in their cell

Stellar Manipulation
--------------------
A full suite of single-use ship components for reshaping the galaxy:
- Destroy or create storms, planets (up to Huge size), and warp points
- Create a new star in a starless system, or destroy one
- Collapse a star into a black hole (destroys everything, including your ship)
- Collapse a black hole back to normal space
- Convert a star system into a nebula, or dissipate an existing nebula
- All stellar manipulation components are consumed on use
- Every empire that has explored the affected system gets a notification

Transport Ships
---------------
- Three new transport chassis: Small, Medium, and Large
- Designed for cargo and troop bays — not combat capable
- Each race now has unique transport ship art for all three sizes

New Components
--------------
- Multiplex Tracking (II-V): Engage 2-5 different targets per combat round
  instead of just one. After destroying a target, remaining weapons retarget
  automatically. A major upgrade for heavily-armed warships.
- Solar Collectors (I-III): Generate 5/10/20 supplies per turn from starlight.
  Output scales with the number of stars in the system.
- Quantum Reactors (I-III): Generate 100/250/500 supplies per turn anywhere,
  even in deep space. Essential for long-range operations.
- Supply Storage expanded to 5 tiers (was 3) for finer control over capacity

Combat Improvements
-------------------
- Cloaked ships now decloak when firing, adding a tactical decision layer
- Multiplex tracking lets ships retarget after a kill in the same round
- Planetary shields absorb bombardment damage before it hits facilities
- Transport ships deploy troops during the troop landing combat phase
- Chassis size affects evasion: corvettes get +40% natural evasion,
  large ships and bases are easier to hit

Star & Galaxy Generation
-------------------------
- Blue Giant and Red Giant star types added to the generation pool
- Stars now have spectral class, corona colors, and luminosity data
- Special systems (dead, pulsar, magnetar, nebula) show as diamonds on
  the galaxy map
- Nebula systems display rich, layered gas cloud visuals filling the system
- Pulsar beams, magnetar surges, and magnetar charge glow are all visible
  as dynamic effects in the system view

Emergency Save

  so you won't lose your progress to an unexpected error
